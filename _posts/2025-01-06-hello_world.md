## Introduction

Building a portfolio website was a task I put off for a long time, thinking it would be a daunting and time-consuming project. However, once I broke it down into smaller steps, the whole process became much more manageable. In this blog post, I’ll walk you through how I built my portfolio website, the tools I used, and some key takeaways from the process.

---

## Step 1: Planning and Design

Before jumping into the code, I spent some time thinking about what I wanted my portfolio to showcase. Here’s a breakdown of the sections I wanted to include:

- **Introduction/About Me**: A brief description of who I am and what I do.
- **Skills**: A list of technologies and tools I am proficient in.
- **Projects**: A section showcasing my best work.
- **Experience**: A summary of my work experience and relevant internships.
- **Contact Information**: How potential clients or employers can reach me.

I used a **wireframe** to design the layout of the website. This was a simple sketch of where each section would go and how they would be organized on the page. Once the design was ready, I felt much more confident about the structure of the site.

---

## Step 2: Choosing the Technology

I wanted to keep things simple, so I chose to use **Jekyll** for building the website. Jekyll is a static site generator that is perfect for creating portfolios, blogs, and documentation sites. It works seamlessly with **GitHub Pages**, so hosting it is free and easy.

### Technologies I Used:
- **HTML5**: For the structure of the pages.
- **CSS3**: For styling, with a focus on a clean and modern design.
- **JavaScript**: For small interactive features like a smooth scroll effect.
- **Jekyll**: For the backend of the site, managing content and layouts.
- **GitHub Pages**: For free hosting of the website.
